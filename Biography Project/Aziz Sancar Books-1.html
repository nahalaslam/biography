Books by Aziz Sancar

Delve into the intellectual world of Aziz Sancar, the accomplished biochemist and Nobel laureate, through his insightful books. Beyond the lab coat, Sancar's written works serve as a gateway to the complexities of molecular biology, offering a lucid exploration of DNA repair mechanisms. With clarity and depth, his literary contributions unravel the intricacies of life at the molecular level, showcasing Sancar's dedication to scientific dissemination. Whether you're a seasoned researcher or an eager learner, Sancar's books provide a captivating journey into the profound ideas that have shaped the landscape of molecular biology.

Aziz Sancar ve Nobel'in Öyküsü

by Aziz Sancar

Read More

"Aziz Sancar ve Nobel'in Öyküsü" is a book written by Turkish author and journalist, Mehmet Özkan. The book tells the story of Aziz Sancar, a Turkish scientist who was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 2015.

The book provides a detailed account of Sancar's life and career, starting from his childhood in a small village in southeastern Turkey, to his studies at Istanbul University and his subsequent move to the United States to pursue graduate studies. The book also highlights Sancar's groundbreaking research on DNA repair mechanisms, which earned him the Nobel Prize.

Throughout the book, Özkan explores the challenges and obstacles that Sancar faced as a scientist from a developing country, as well as the role that politics and international relations played in his career. The book also touches upon Sancar's personal life, including his marriage to another prominent Turkish scientist, Prof. Dr. Tülin Özkan-Sancar.

Özkan's writing style is engaging and informative, making the book accessible to both scientists and non-scientists alike. The book also includes numerous photographs and illustrations that help to bring Sancar's story to life.

Overall, "Aziz Sancar ve Nobel'in Öyküsü" is an inspiring and insightful biography that sheds light on the life and work of one of Turkey's most accomplished scientists. It is a testament to the power of hard work, dedication, and perseverance, and serves as a role model for young scientists both in Turkey and around the world.
