Abdus Salam Biography


"Scientific thought and its creation is the common and shared heritage of mankind".

Abdus Salam was a Pakistani physicist and professor who was born in the town of Jhang in 1926. At the age of 14, Salam left his hometown to attend the Government College in Lahore, where he scored higher on the entrance exam than any candidate in the school's history.He completed his undergraduate studies at Punjab University and subsequently received a doctorate from Cambridge University in 1952.In 1979, Salam became the first Muslim physicist to scientist to win a Nobel Prize, which he shared with two American physicists, Dr. Steven Weinberg and Dr. Sheldon Glashow. They won the prize for their work on the electroweak theory.Dr. Abdus Salam was a devout Ahmadi Muslim, a proud citizen of Pakistan, a Nobel Prize winning physicist and a committed supporter of global science, particularly in regions underrepresented in the international scientific community.

Aziz Sancar Biography

"Discovering the Molecular Dance:
Aziz Sancar's Path to Excellence"


Aziz Sancar, born in Turkey in 1946, is a distinguished biochemist renowned for his groundbreaking contributions to DNA repair mechanisms. A Nobel laureate in Chemistry (2015), Sancar's pioneering work has deepened our understanding of the intricate processes that safeguard our genetic information. His remarkable career stands as a testament to the pursuit of scientific excellence in unraveling the mysteries of molecular biology. Educated at Istanbul University Faculty of Medicine, Sancar's early years laid the foundation for a remarkable trajectory. Venturing to the United States for further studies, he pursued a Master's degree in Molecular and Cell Biology at the University of Texas at Dallas, followed by a Ph.D. in Molecular and Cell Biology at the University of Texas Medical Branch (UTMB) at Galveston.

Ahmed Zewail Biography

Investing In Science Education And Curiosity-Driven Research Is Investing In The Future.


Ahmed Zewail was an Egyptian and American chemist,known as the "father of femtochemistry"He was awarded the 1999 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on femtochemistry and became the first Egyptian and Arab to win a Nobel Prize in a scientific feild and the second African to win a Nobel Prize in chemistry.He was the Linus Pauling Chair Professor Of Chemistry,a professor of physics,and the director of the Physical Biology Center for Ultrafast Science and Technology at the Calfornia Institute of Technology. Ahmed Zewail's revolutionary contributions extended to his role as the Linus Pauling Chair Professor of Chemistry, a Professor of Physics, and the director of the Physical Biology Center for Ultrafast Science and Technology at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech). Beyond the accolades, Zewail's scientific prowess earned him the moniker "father of femtochemistry" for his pioneering work in the field.

ABDUS SALAM

The Abdus Salam Award (sometimes called the Salam Prize)'is a most prestigious award that is awarded annually to Pakistani nationals to the feild of chemistry ,mathematics ,physics and biology. The award is awarded to the scientists who are resident in Pakistan, below 35 years of age on 31 December of the year for which the Prize was to be awarded. It is to consist of a certificate giving a citation and a cash award of US$1,000.It is to be awarded on the basis of the collected research and/or a technical essay written specially for the Prize.
